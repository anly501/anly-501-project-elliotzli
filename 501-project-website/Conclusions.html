Conclusions

In this project, I explored mental health statistics in the past 10 years. I collected creditable data from various sources with quantitative data and Twitter with text data. In the tab Exploring Data, I analyzed the trends of mental health topics among different populations, such as the number of mental health illnesses, the number of receiving mental health services, and the attempted suicide rate, and used Twitter API to generate online sentiments around young people’s mental health-related topics. As I established basic facts about the seriousness of mental health issues in the US, I moved on to use more individual data for prediction purposes. In Naive Bayes, I explored the best way to predict Twitter sentiment. In Decision Tree, I attempted to predict how music affects one’s mental health. In SVM, I explored suicidal behaviors among adolescents. In Clustering, I attempted to cluster the dataset by clustering approaches rather than self-identified music effects. Overall, I enjoyed applying course concepts to real-life situations, and it raised my awareness of how the importance of mental health. There’s more to explore on the topic, and I’d like to revisit the project when I have a better understanding of different data science skills.
